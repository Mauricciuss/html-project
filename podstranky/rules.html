Magic is a game about casting spells to lower your opponent's life total to 0 before they can do the same to you. This section highlights some of the most common in-game actions with visual aid, as well as other useful rules information.

CASTING SPELLS

To cast a spell, you must pay its mana cost (located in the upper right corner of the card) by tapping lands (or other permanents) to make the amount and type of mana which that spell requires. Below is a visual repsresentation of this game action.

ATTACKING AND BLOCKING

Players attack using the creature cards they have played. Each creature card has two numbers in the corner of the card that are used for attacking and blocking - power on the left and toughness on the right. To attack with a creature, tap the creature by rotating the card sideways. It is now attacking. A player can choose to block the attacking creature with one of their untapped creatures or to take damage from the attacker. Both cases are shown below.

FORMATS OF PLAY

Different styles of playing the game exist, with varying rules. Each format has different specifications for minimal deck size, number of players and other aspects of play. The following table displays the most prevalent formats along with their different stipulations.

Format Players Deck Size
Limited 2 40+ cards
Standard 2 60+ cards
Two-Headed Giant 4 40+ cards
Commander 4 100 cards exactly
